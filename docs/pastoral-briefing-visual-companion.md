# Formation & Transition — Pastoral Briefing Visual Companion

## Purpose

This is the only material that goes on-screen during the pastoral briefing described in *Formation & Transition — Pastoral Briefing Guide*. It holds visual anchors only — Greek vocabulary, the sequence chain, and the movement titles — not sentences to read aloud. The conversation stays spoken and internalized; this exists only for the handful of details that are easier to absorb seen than heard.

*When used:* Screen-shared during the live Zoom conversation — pulled up when introducing a term, walking through the sequence chain, or orienting the pastor on where the conversation is in the arc.

*Who uses it:* Andrew, sharing his screen with the pastor.

## The Sequence

**paideia → hexis → regulatory capacity → sympatheia → allēlōn functions**

Formation produces disposition. Disposition produces the capacity to stay non-anxious in another's suffering. That capacity is what lets the body's one-another functions actually operate. Structure does not produce this chain — it can only house it once the chain is already running.
